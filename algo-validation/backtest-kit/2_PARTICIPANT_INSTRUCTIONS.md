# Participant Instructions (give this to the person — keep the real purpose hidden)

## What to say when recruiting
> "We're building a free tool that suggests office/desk career directions for people early in
> their careers, and we're testing whether the questions make sense. It takes about 5 minutes.
> Could you try it and answer honestly based on how you actually are — not how you think you
> should answer?"

**Do NOT tell them** the test is checking whether the tool can find the job they already do. If
they know, they'll answer to match their current job and the result will look better than it is.

## Instructions to read out / send
1. Answer every question based on your **natural preference and comfort**, not what sounds
   impressive and not based on your current job title.
2. There are no right or wrong answers. Pick what genuinely fits you.
3. If two options both feel true, pick the one that's *slightly* more you.
4. Don't overthink — go with your first honest instinct.
5. Answer in whichever language (English / Hindi) feels easiest.

## What the facilitator records (NOT shown to the participant)
Fill these into the scoring sheet **before and after** they take it:

- **Before:** their real current job title, and which of the 12 roles it maps to. Decide this
  mapping *first* — don't change it after seeing the engine's answer.
- **After:** the engine's Top 1, Top 2, Top 3 roles, and the confidence margin.

## Tips for a clean run
- Sit them somewhere they won't feel judged — honest answers need a relaxed setting.
- If they ask "which job are you testing me for?", deflect: "any of them — just answer honestly."
- Run it on the same device/build for everyone so you're testing one version of the engine.
- One sitting per person; don't let them redo it to "get a better result."
